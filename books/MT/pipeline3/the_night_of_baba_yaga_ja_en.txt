I

On the Kōshū Kaidō, with dusk beginning to fall, the white sedan reeked of cigarettes and blood.

In the back seat, wedged between a man in a gaudy tie and another in a patterned shirt, a long-haired woman sat slumped with her head hanging. She wore torn jeans and nothing but a cheap T-shirt, and her limp, outflung hands were grimy. When the car jolted over a bump, a thick drop of blood plopped onto the belly of her yellow T-shirt. The men kept glancing at it, looking uncomfortable. The young driver, too, kept shooting uneasy looks at the woman in the rearview mirror every time he stopped at a light.

The sedan tailed a Ford just ahead. The Ford's polished black body shone slick in the streetlights and neon beginning to come on. The sunset that evening was brutal. The glass windows of buildings all over Shinjuku burned red, as if the towers themselves were bleeding from every inch.

Before long the two cars entered a quiet residential neighborhood in Setagaya. A trim, placid streetscape spread around them, too neat to seem like the same Tokyo as cluttered Shinjuku. But two, three corners later, a high stone wall suddenly loomed up, as if to intimidate everything around it. The Shirakawa stone was stacked tight, taller than a man, with barbed wire strung along the top. The wall ringed the grounds in a broad sweep, as if built to hide something. When the cars stopped in front of it, an iron gate bristling with surveillance cameras opened after a moment.

Inside the wall sat an enormous single-story mansion and a magnificent Japanese garden. There was even a small five-story pagoda in one corner. The cars moved slowly along the stone-paved drive and stopped near the pagoda.

Before anyone knew it, men had appeared from somewhere and gathered around the cars, boxing them in. All of them were young, hard-faced, dressed alike in white shirts and matching patterned ties.

A man jumped out of the Ford's driver's seat and opened the rear door. Mirror-bright shoes slid out first, then the long narrow legs wearing them. A man in a funeral-black suit stepped out. He was tall, with cheeks pared hollow as if carved away and one flattened ear.

At a jerk of his chin, the white sedan's door flew open and the men inside began dragging the limp woman out. She was long-limbed, not fat but built thick and solid; even with two of them, hauling her out looked like work.

They dumped her on the flagstones like luggage, and her back jerked once. But she stayed facedown, not getting up.

"Hey. You still alive?"

When the man in the black suit said it, the man in the patterned shirt hurriedly drove a hard kick into her shin.

A hoarse groan rose. Still flat to the ground, like a crocodile, the woman slowly lifted her face. Hair stiff with dried blood clung in filthy strands to her cheek, dark blood ran from her nostrils, and the skin beside her right eye was split and swollen to a dark bruise.

"That's one ugly bitch."

The man in the black suit gave a scornful snort, and the men around him joined in with dutiful snickers.

"Get her on her feet."

The man in the patterned shirt nodded, grabbed the woman's left arm, and hauled her up. She leaned on him meekly and rose, swaying.

But then—

"Bwugh."

A cry like a toad being crushed went up. The man in the patterned shirt lifted off the ground, spun once in the air, and slammed down on the flagstones flat on his back.

The men broke into an uproar. The man in the patterned shirt let out a thin whistle of breath, his eyes rolling back till the whites showed, and went still.

This time the woman did not sway. She stood there with her feet spread wide, spread her arms with her palms open, looked the men over with a slow, sticky glare, and opened her mouth wide. Bloody teeth showed inside.

One of the white-shirted men realized she was grinning at them, baiting them; his face twisted, and he lunged straight at her.

"Hss."

A sound sharp enough to cut air leaked from between the woman's teeth. She bent her knees, dropped her head, and charged. The man took the full force of a boar's headbutt in the gut and flew backward, then hit the hard ground on his shoulder before he could even break his fall. A dull pop, the sound of bone coming out of socket. His scream turned high and wild.

A brief, hesitant pause opened up, and then the other men rushed her too.

"Hss."

Her fist sank into the Adam's apple of the man who came at her from the right. Unable to cry out, unable even to breathe, he dropped onto his ass where he stood and kicked his legs wildly. The man charging in from the left took a brutal kick from a steel-toed boot straight to the kneecap. The sickening crack of the joint giving way rang clear even through the din of shouting. Another man's fist smashed hard into the woman's cheek. She reeled, staggering a few steps, but quickly reset and brought both arms up to guard her head like a boxer.

"Hss."

She caught the punch of the man swinging at her again on her forearm and, in the same instant, drove the steel-capped toe of her boot up into his groin. A scream. Shouts. More and more white-shirted men came crowding in front of the garage.

Their cheeks were flushed red with anger and tension, and yet something in their faces looked almost dreamlike. As if they could not take in what was right in front of them, could not believe what was happening. Amid that anger and bewilderment, only the blood-smeared woman bared her teeth and smiled. Still smiling, she never stopped punching and kicking.

Through the thick curses came the bark of a dog. A black-and-brown blur shot through a gap in the pack of white shirts. A huge Doberman with a thick black leather collar around its neck went straight for the woman. Forty kilos of charging dog hit her head-on and sent her to the ground. The men swarmed her at once, trying to pin her body to the ground. Growls from man and dog. The sound of cloth ripping.

"Hey, don't kill her. She's a gift for the boss. Handle her carefully."

The man in the black suit watched, amused. The sun went down. Night fell.

At the center of an elegant garden laid with ornamental gravel, the woman was pinned to the ground by four man-catchers. The men holding them had not come away unhurt themselves, and with hateful creases cut between their brows, they glared down at the woman flattened against the earth. Her clothes and hair were even more in tatters than before, but she did not move, did not groan, only breathed quietly face-down. Her shoes had been stripped off, and the black of her underwear showing through the shredded T-shirt looked like a wounded tiger. In the stillness of early evening came the splash of koi in the pond.

"Yanagi, what's that. A woman?"

The voice was roughened by drink. On a leather sofa on the veranda facing the garden sat a man in a striped yukata. Mid-sixties, maybe. Bald, belly bulging, his short, boar-thick neck buried in sloping shoulders.

"Looks female, at least. I thought she might be good for that job, so I brought her in."

The black-suited man called Yanagi took an old orange leather wallet from his pocket and pulled out the driver's license.

"Yoriko Shindō... twenty-two. From Hokkaido."

"What is she?"

"Things got noisy out front of the office, so I sent one of the young guys to see what was going on, and this was out there tearing the place up. Rare creature, so I figured I'd scout it."

At that, the man on the veranda laughed in a grating voice. A vivid traditional tattoo showed at the open collar of his yukata.

"Scout it, huh."

"I had to take the liberty of giving her two or three cracks with a beer bottle before she'd be kind enough to accompany us. We'll dig into who she is properly later, but her brawling strength is the real deal. She took on ten pros at once and was still standing like it was nothing. If she weren't female, I'd want her as one of my boys."

"She really is a woman, right? These days plenty look like women and still have cocks."

"We'll check that too, nice and thorough, later."

A round of vulgar laughter went up from the men.

Shindō listened to the exchange in silence, her gaze fixed on the man on the veranda. She wasn't glaring. She wasn't pleading for anything. She was simply looking at him.

"While you're at it, wash her good, inside and out, and clean her up. Don't put her in front of that looking all filthy."

With that, the man on the veranda got to his feet as if annoyed by the effort and withdrew into the house. Young men in white shirts materialized at once like stagehands and cleared away the ashtrays and chairs.

"Hey. Get a hose. Turn it on."

Grabbing the hose dragged over from somewhere in the garden, Yanagi started hosing Shindō down, slapping her with the cold water bursting from it.

"What, quiet all of a sudden? Finally ready to give in?"

The stream slammed into her blood-slick face. Shindō opened her mouth and drank it greedily, gulp after gulp, as if it tasted good.

Yanagi laughed.

"You've got some balls. Or are you too stupid to understand what's happening to you?"

The water stopped. The cold spray had washed the blood from her face, but the fresh bruises she'd taken in the brawl had swollen worse than ever, to the point that it was hard to imagine what her features had originally looked like.

"If you're stupid, so much the better. Listen, little Yoriko. Starting today, you work at this estate. If you don't like it, I'll kill you right here. Understand?"

Shindō said nothing, only nodded once.

"Good. Up till now, this was just fun and games. From this second on, if you go against me, you die. Got that?"

Yanagi eased open his suit jacket. A dirk in a pale wooden sheath was tucked at his waist. Shindō nodded again.

"Back off."

At Yanagi's signal, the men with the man-catchers drew the metal restraints slowly away from Shindō's body.

"On your feet."

Without the slightest wobble, Shindō rose straight to her feet. The skin showing through her torn clothes was covered in fresh cuts and bruises, but she made no move to suggest she was holding pain in check.

"You heard what was said earlier. Strip. Let me see whether you're actually female."

Without a word, Shindō pulled off the T-shirt, already little more than a rag, and tossed it to the ground.

"Underwear too."

She stripped off the black bra without the slightest hesitation. A few of the men around her were grinning, but what drew the eye wasn't so much her bared breasts as the magnificent ridges of muscle beneath them. The arms the T-shirt had hidden were dense with muscle too, thick and bulging. Her body was like one of the guardian kings at Tōdai-ji's Great South Gate, as if hewn out of a giant tree.

"Take off the panties too. That's what matters."

Without taking her eyes off Yanagi, Shindō started peeling off her button-fly jeans. A large ass sheathed in black underwear came into view, along with taut thighs. All the men's eyes fixed there.

The next instant, Shindō swung the soaked jeans high and cracked them across Yanagi's face like a whip.

"Shit!"

The wet, stiff denim slammed into his head. In the instant before Yanagi could bat it away, Shindō launched herself straight at him. She crashed into his chest as if clinging to him, grabbed the lapel of his suit with her left hand, and snatched the dirk from his waist with her right. At the same moment, Yanagi moved. He seized her by the hair, yanked her in, and drove her to the ground with a smooth outer reap. He tried to kick away the hand holding the dirk, but Shindō rolled clear of it and sprang back up at once.

"Get her!"

Somebody barked the order. The Doberman from before obeyed its master, bared its white fangs, and lunged at her half-naked body. Shindō instantly snatched up the jeans from the ground, whipped them around, wrapped them around her arm, and thrust it out in front of the dog.

With a growl, it clamped onto her arm through the wet denim. Its jaws kept grinding down with the kind of force that could tear flesh and break bone on an ordinary person, locked by a fierce refusal to let go. The more she tried to shake it off, the more stubbornly it held on.

The beast and the beastlike woman locked eyes.

Shindō bared her teeth and let out a harsh bark. Showing which of them was the stronger animal. The dog did not stop biting, but for an instant a flicker of fear surfaced in those big wet black eyes. Then, with forty kilos hanging off her and slowing her down, man-catchers came stabbing in at her body from every side and forced her back to the ground. At the command to stop, the dog let go at once and returned to its master.

As he straightened his mussed hair with his fingers, Yanagi spat on the ground. Then he brought a leather shoe down on Shindō's wrist, the dirk still clenched in her hand.

"You some kind of lunatic? Everyone here is the real deal, actual yakuza. Doesn't dying scare you?"

Yanagi's cruel-looking face wore a smile that said he was enjoying this too much.

"Don't get familiar with me, you piece of shit."

"Oh? So you can talk."

Yanagi bore down harder on her wrist. The dirk clattered out of her palm.

"You... why didn't you stab the dog?"

Shindō grimaced and spat out a gob of blood-tinged saliva.

"Nishi. Bring that dog here."

The man in the white shirt called Nishi nodded and dragged over the Doberman, a lead clipped to its collar. Yanagi picked up the dirk.

"I'll say it one more time. Starting today, you work here. Say no and I'll kill you right here."

He pointed the tip of the blade at Shindō's face.

"Do what you want. I'd rather die than take orders from you."

"I see."

With that, Yanagi seized the collar of the dog, which had been sitting there docilely wagging its tail, and pressed the blade to its throat. Shindō's eyes flew open.

"Stop."

A thin little whine escaped it. A powerful animal, one that could kill a human with a single bite if it chose, trembled helplessly as its own life was threatened.

"Can't hear you."

The Doberman's tail tucked tighter, and its thin, high whine of fear swelled.

"Stop!"

Its big ears flattened against its head, and its black eyes flew wide and round.

"So you're saying I can kill it. This one dies because of you. Poor thing."

He pressed the blade in. The dog's wet eyes stared straight at Shindō.

"I'll do what you say."

"What was that?"

"I'll do what you say!"

Yanagi's hand came off the collar at once. The dog gave a small whine, sank sadly to the ground, and looked up at him from under its brow. There was no resentment in its eyes. No anger.

"You can crush a man's balls, but you feel sorry for the doggie? Aren't you sweet, Yoriko. Listen. If you ever go against me again, or try to run from this place, I'll slit this thing's belly open and skin it alive. Right in front of you."

With that, Yanagi slid the clean dirk back into the sheath at his waist.

On the covered passage, polished bright enough to mirror her face, Shindō knelt alone. Her reddish-brown hair had been pulled back with an office rubber band, and she wore a baggy white shirt and black pants that didn't fit. Gauze and adhesive bandages had been plastered over her face, enough to hide everything but her eyes, nose, and mouth, leaving her looking like a botched mummy. It was less treatment than concealment, a way to cover the injuries and nothing more.

While she changed into clothes borrowed from the man in the white shirt, one of the live-in recruits, Yanagi had done all the talking. This was the estate of Genzō Uchiki, boss of the Uchiki-kai, a direct affiliate of the Okitsu-gumi, the largest yakuza syndicate in Kantō. Yanagi served as the Uchiki-kai underboss's lieutenant. Every chore in the estate, from sweeping to cooking, was handled by handpicked young men from the subordinate outfits, but for one particular job they absolutely needed a woman, so they had "scouted" Shindō.

They had told her they'd explain the work soon enough, so she was to sit and wait. More than thirty minutes had passed. It was dark, and there wasn't a soul in sight. The nearest stone wall was maybe twenty meters away. If she wanted to, she could be gone in seconds. But Shindō couldn't make herself move.

That Doberman's thin cry, full of despair and resignation, still rang in her ears.

Dogs aren't guilty of anything. No matter how vile the human who owns them, dogs aren't guilty. Those eyes... full of despair, and still not resisting the one holding their life in his hands. That pitiful whine. All dogs are like that. Somehow they pledge loyalty even to hopeless human beings.

When she sighed, pain ran along her ribs. Nothing felt broken, but there might be a crack. She thought back over what had happened that day.

For one of her two part-time jobs, she rode into Shinjuku on her bike and spent the day delivering meals until evening. She went back to her apartment in Shin-Ōkubo, left the bike there, washed off the sweat, changed clothes, and headed into Shinjuku again, this time on foot. If all she wanted was something to eat, she could have stayed near home, but she thought she might catch a movie while she was out. But no sooner had she entered Kabukichō than a drunk punk, cackling to himself, slapped Shindō on the ass as he brushed past; she spun on her heel, grabbed him by the back of the collar from behind, swept his legs, and drove him face-first into the asphalt. It was almost funny that his buddy walked on a few more paces before he noticed, but soon enough he came scrambling back and swung a wild haymaker; she could have dodged it if she had wanted to, but a crowd had already started to gather, and she figured it would be less trouble if she let herself get hit once, so she took it on purpose, right in the face. Whether he was drunk or just that bad, the punch barely even made her skin itch; she caught his wrist from underneath and wrenched it hard inward. A nasty little crunch shivered through her hand, not a pleasant feeling, and the joint came neatly out on the first try. A scream. Panicking, the man started hammering at her stomach with his uninjured hand, so she let go, kicked him away, and opened up some distance. The gawkers multiplied in no time; the man she had dropped onto the asphalt was rolling around sluggishly, groaning, blood pouring from his nose. She still wanted to get in a few more hits, but they gave her nothing back, and having the cops called would be a nuisance, so she decided to bolt, only to have several obviously rough-looking men step out of the crowd and block her path. More of them, she thought, just as a kick came flying at her. It was a high kick, a lot better than anything the punk had managed. As the man shouted "fucking bitch," "pig," and whatever else and kept kicking at her, she slipped the second, the third, and caught the fourth. He was short. She stomped on his planted foot to pin it, hoisted the other leg onto her shoulder, and dropped hard into a squat, forcing his hip open into a hundred-and-eighty-degree split. A scream tore out of him like a newborn's. When she straightened up, something slammed into her back, probably a flying kick. She turned and saw the man who had kicked her stumbling the instant he landed, so she snapped a high kick into the wide-open side of his neck. It made a good, sharp smack. Then pain flared along her side, and when she looked, somebody had caught her with a sideways swing of an empty wine bottle. That was probably the injury throbbing now. Once weapons came out in a place this cramped, the odds turned bad. A bystander could get hurt too. She looked for a way out, but the guy with the bottle hauled back for another swing, so she drove her fist straight into his face. Through the gaps in the ring of gawkers around her, she could feel several murderous presences drawing near. More. More of them, coming to get punched by me. Shindō found herself smiling. If this place were bigger, if nobody got in the way, I could take every last one of you. She cursed the city's narrowness, its crush of people. Then something slammed hard into the back of her head. If Yanagi was to be believed, it was a beer bottle. Another blow came right after it, and then blackout.

A night wind, unseasonably chilly for May, swept through. Holding still on the cold, hard hallway floor, she took another deep breath and ran a hand over her throbbing ribs and the back of her head.
