The white sedan driving down the Koshu Kaido as dusk began to fall reeked of cigarette smoke and blood.

In the backseat, sandwiched between a man in a flashy tie and another in a patterned shirt, a woman with long hair sat slumped, her head hanging down. She was dressed in torn jeans and a cheap T-shirt, and her loosely sprawled hands were grimy. The moment the car bounced slightly, a viscous drop of blood fell with a heavy plop onto the belly of her yellow T-shirt. The men cast uncomfortable, sidelong glances at it. The young man driving also threw nervous looks at the woman through the rearview mirror every time they stopped at a light.

The sedan was following close behind a Ford. Its polished black body gleamed wetly, catching the light of the streetlamps and neon signs just starting to flicker on. The sunset that day was intense. The glass windows of every building in Shinjuku were dyed crimson, as if their entire bodies were bleeding.

Before long, the two cars entered a quiet residential neighborhood in Setagaya. A neat and tranquil landscape spread out around them—so much so that it was hard to believe this was the same Tokyo as chaotic Shinjuku. But after turning two, then three corners, an intimidatingly high stone wall abruptly appeared, dominating the surroundings. The Shirakawa stones, stacked tightly without a single gap, rose well above head height, with barbed wire strung along the very top. The cars stopped in front of this wall, which was built to broadly enclose and conceal whatever lay inside, and after a moment, heavy iron gates fitted with imposing security cameras swung open.

Inside the stone wall lay a sprawling single-story mansion and a magnificent Japanese garden. There was even a small five-story pagoda built in the corner. The cars slowly advanced along the stone-paved driveway and stopped near the pagoda.

Before they knew it, men had emerged from somewhere and gathered to surround the cars. They were all young, with grim faces, wearing white dress shirts and matching patterned ties.





A man jumped out of the Ford's driver's seat and opened the rear door. Impeccably polished shoes and the long, slender legs wearing them slid smoothly into view. A man stepped out, wearing a black suit like someone returning from a funeral. He was tall, with conspicuously hollowed cheeks—as if whittled down to the bone—and a crushed ear.

The man jerked his chin, and the doors of the white sedan immediately opened. The men inside began to drag the still-slumped woman out. The woman had long limbs, and though not fat, she had a thick, sturdy build; dragging her out didn't look like an easy task, even for two men.

Dropped unceremoniously onto the paving stones like a piece of luggage, the woman's back jolted. But she remained face down, not getting up.

"Hey, is she alive?"

When the man in the black suit spoke, the man in the patterned shirt hastily delivered a hard kick to the woman's shin.

A hoarse groan escaped her. Still sprawled on the ground, she slowly raised her head like a crocodile. Hair clumped with dried blood clung filthily to her cheek, dark red blood oozed from her nostrils, and a gash next to her right eye was swollen a bruised, purplish-black.

"What an ugly bitch." 

The man in the black suit snorted, and the surrounding men chuckled in sycophantic agreement.

"Get her up."

The man in the patterned shirt nodded, grabbing the woman's left arm and hoisting her up. She leaned into it submissively, staggering to her feet. But—

"Bweh!"

An instant later, a sound like a crushed toad rang out. The man in the patterned shirt was launched into the air, spun full circle, and slammed back-first onto the paving stones. 

The men murmured in shock. The man in the patterned shirt let out a thin, whistling breath; his eyes rolled back into his head, and he went completely still.

This time, the woman didn't stagger; she stood planted firmly on the spot. Opening her hands and spreading her arms, she swept a lingering, menacing glare over the men and opened her mouth wide. Blood-smeared teeth showed.





Realizing that this was a mocking smile meant to provoke them, one of the white-shirted men twisted his face in rage and lunged straight at her.

"Ssst."

A sharp hiss that seemed to slice the air leaked through the woman’s teeth. She swiftly bent her knees, lowered her head, and charged. Taking a fierce, boar-like headbutt squarely in the stomach, the man was effortlessly sent flying, crashing shoulder-first onto the hard ground before he could even break his fall. There was a dull *pop* of a bone dislocating. A strangled scream tore through the air.

For an instant, there was a hesitant pause, and then the other men also lunged at the woman.

"Ssst."

She buried her fist into the Adam's apple of a man leaping in from the right. Unable to voice a sound, let alone breathe, he collapsed onto his backside, legs flailing. A steel-toed kick smashed into the kneecap of the man rushing her from the left. The sickening crunch of a joint giving way rang out clearly, even over the roaring shouts. Another man's fist struck her hard across the cheek. She staggered back a few steps but instantly recovered her footing, raising both arms to guard her head like a boxer.

"Ssst."

She blocked another incoming punch with her arm, simultaneously driving her steel-clad toe into the man's groin. Screams. Bellows of rage. A swarm of white-shirted men poured out, gathering in front of the garage.

The men's faces were flushed with fury and tension, yet they all wore dazed expressions, as if trapped in a dream. They looked as though they simply couldn't process or believe what was happening right in front of their eyes. Amidst the anger and bewilderment, only the blood-soaked woman was baring her teeth in a grin. Grinning, she kept punching and kicking endlessly.





Mixed with the deep-throated curses, the barking of a dog could be heard. A blur of black and tan burst through a gap in the swarm of white shirts. A massive Doberman, wearing a thick black leather collar, lunged straight at the woman. Taking the forty-kilo charge head-on, she went down. Instantly, the men swarmed her, moving to pin her body down. The snarling of men and dog. The sound of tearing cloth.

"Hey, don't kill her. She's a gift for the boss. Handle her with care." 

The man in the black suit watched the spectacle in amusement. The sun dipped below the horizon, and night fell.

In the center of the elegant, gravel-paved garden, the woman was pinned to the ground by four *sasumata* poles. The men wielding them were far from unscathed themselves; their brows were furrowed in pure hatred as they glared down at the woman sprawled on the ground. Her clothes and hair were now utterly shredded, but she lay motionless on her stomach, making no sound, breathing steadily. Her shoes had been removed, and the black underwear exposed beneath her torn T-shirt made her look like a wounded tiger. In the evening stillness, the splash of a leaping carp echoed from the pond.

"Yanagi, what the hell is that? A woman?" 

A raspy, liquor-scorched voice rang out. A man in a striped yukata sat on a leather sofa set out on the veranda overlooking the garden. He looked to be in his mid-sixties. He was bald, with a bulging gut, his short, thick neck sinking into sloping shoulders.

"Seems to be a female, anyway. I brought her in thinking she might be useful for that job." 

The man in the black suit, called Yanagi, pulled a worn orange leather wallet from his pocket. He slipped a driver’s license out of it.





"Shindo Yoriko... Twenty-two years old. Born in Hokkaido."

"Who is she?"

"There was a commotion out front of the office, so I sent one of the boys to take a look, and found this one going wild. She was a rare creature, so I scouted her."

When Yanagi said this, the man on the veranda let out a grating laugh. Vibrant Japanese tattoos peeked from his open collar.

"Scouted her, did you?"

"I did have to crack her with a beer bottle a few times before she'd agree to accompany us, though. We'll dig into her background later, but her brawling strength is the real deal. She took on ten pros at once and remained standing like it was nothing. If she wasn't a female, I'd almost want to make her one of my boys."

"You sure she's really a woman? Plenty of 'em out there looking like women but packing a dick these days."

"I'll be sure to inspect that thoroughly later."

Vulgar chuckles rippled among the men.

The woman—Shindo Yoriko—listened to their exchange in silence, her gaze fixed squarely on the man on the veranda. She wasn't glaring at him, nor was she pleading; she was simply watching him.

"While you're checking, wash her out thoroughly and clean her up. Don't put her in front of *him* looking so filthy." 

With that, the man on the veranda stood up with a heavy grunt and retreated into the house. White-shirted young men instantly materialized like stagehands to clear away his ashtray and chair.

"Hey, bring the hose. Turn on the water." 

Grabbing a hose dragged over from somewhere in the garden, Yanagi began splashing Shindo with a torrent of cold water.

"What, you're quiet now. Finally given up?" 

The high-pressure stream blasted against her blood-smeared face. Shindo opened her mouth and gulped the water down with obvious relish.





Yanagi chuckled. 

"You've got some nerve. Or maybe you're just too slow in the head to understand what's going on."

The water stopped. The cold spray had washed away the blood, but her face was swelling rapidly from the fresh bruises she'd taken in the brawl, making it hard to even guess at her original features.

"If you're an idiot, all the better for us. Listen to me, Yoriko-chan. Starting today, you're going to work at this estate. If you refuse, I'll kill you right here, right now. Do you understand?" 

Shindo remained silent, offering a single nod.

"Good. Everything up until now was just playtime. But from this moment forward, if you defy me, you die. Got it?" 

Yanagi pulled open his suit jacket. Tucked into his waistband was a dagger in a plain wooden sheath. Shindo nodded again.

"Back off." 

Yanagi signaled to the men holding the *sasumata*, and the metal restraints slowly lifted from Shindo's body.

"Stand up."

Without a wobble, Shindo rose to her feet. The skin visible through her shredded clothes was covered in fresh cuts and dark bruises, yet she showed no sign of enduring pain.

"You heard what we were saying earlier. Strip. Let's see if you're really a female."

In silence, Shindo pulled off her T-shirt, now nothing more than a ragged scrap of cloth, and tossed it to the dirt.

"The underwear, too."

Without a hint of hesitation, she took off her black bra and tossed it aside. A few of the surrounding men smirked, but their eyes were quickly drawn away from her exposed breasts to the awe-inspiring, perfectly sculpted six-pack beneath them. Her arms, previously hidden by the T-shirt, were packed with dense, bulging muscle. It was a physique reminiscent of the fearsome guardian deities at Todai-ji Temple's Great South Gate—a body that looked as if it had been carved from a massive tree.

"Your panties, too. That's the most important part."





Without breaking eye contact with Yanagi, Shindo began unfastening her button-fly jeans. They fell away, revealing a large ass encased in black panties and a pair of taut thighs. Every man's gaze homed in on them.

In the next fraction of a second, Shindo swung her heavy, wet jeans like a whip, lashing them directly across Yanagi's face.

"Shit!"

The stiff, wet denim smacked hard against his head. In the split second it took Yanagi to bat it away, Shindo leapt straight forward. She dove into his chest as if to cling to him, grabbing the lapel of his suit with her left hand while her right hand snatched the dagger from his waistband. But at the exact same moment, Yanagi moved. He grabbed a fistful of Shindo's hair, jerked her close, and took her down to the ground with a fluid *osoto gari* leg sweep. He instantly tried to kick away the hand holding the dagger, but Shindo rolled to dodge it and immediately sprang back up.

"Go!" 

A command flew through the air. The Doberman from earlier obeyed its master's order, baring its white fangs as it lunged at her half-naked body. Shindo instantly snatched up her discarded jeans from the ground, swiftly wrapped them around her arm, and thrust it out in front of the dog's eyes.

With a vicious snarl, the dog clamped its jaws around her arm over the wet jeans. It bit down with enough force to tear the flesh and shatter the bones of an ordinary person, holding on with a ferocious determination never to let go. When she tried to shake it off, the dog only bit down harder.

The beast and the beast-like woman locked eyes.

Shindo bared her teeth and unleashed a guttural roar, asserting which of them was the stronger creature. The dog didn't let go, but a flicker of fear flashed in its large, wet black eyes for an instant. But burdened by a forty-kilo weight, Shindo’s movements had slowed, and *sasumata* poles flew at her torso from all directions, pinning her to the ground once again.
At the command to "stop,"





the dog instantly released its bite and trotted back to its master.

Smoothing back his disheveled hair with his fingers, Yanagi spat on the ground. He stomped his leather shoe down hard on Shindo's wrist, which still gripped the dagger.

"Are you crazy? Every man here is a real yakuza. Aren't you afraid of dying?"

Yanagi's cruel face wore a smile that showed he found this endlessly amusing.

"Don't act so familiar, you piece of trash."

"Oh, so you can speak."

Yanagi ground his heel harder into her wrist. The dagger clattered from her palm.

"You..." Yanagi said. "Why didn't you stab the dog?"

Shindo contorted her face and spat a glob of blood-streaked saliva.

"Nishi, bring the dog over here."

The white-shirted man called Nishi nodded and pulled the Doberman over by a leash attached to its collar. Yanagi picked up the dagger.

"I'll say it one more time. Starting today, you work here. Say no, and I'll kill you right now."

He pointed the tip of the blade at Shindo's face.

"Do what you want. I'd rather die than do as you say."

"Is that right."

With that, Yanagi grabbed the collar of the dog, which had been sitting quietly, wagging its tail, and pressed the blade against its throat. Shindo’s eyes went wide.

"...Stop."

A pitiful whine slipped from the dog. This powerful creature—an animal capable of killing a human with a single bite if it wanted to—was trembling, completely unable to resist as its life was threatened.

"I can't hear you."

The Doberman tucked its tail, and its high-pitched whine, crying out in terror, grew louder.

"Stop it!"

Its large ears flattened as if crushed, and its black eyes widened into perfect circles.

"So I can kill it, then? It's going to die because of you. Poor thing."

He dug the blade in. The dog's wet eyes stared intently at Shindo.





"I'll do what you want."

"What was that?"

"I'll do what you say!"

Yanagi abruptly released the collar. With a soft whine, the dog dropped miserably to the ground and looked up at Yanagi with upturned eyes. There was no trace of resentment or anger in those eyes.

"You can crush a man's balls, but you feel sorry for a doggy? How sweet of you, Yoriko-chan. Listen to me. If you ever defy me or try to escape from here again, I'll slice this mutt's belly open and skin it alive. Right in front of your eyes."

With that, Yanagi slid the bloodless dagger back into the sheath at his waist.

Shindo sat alone in *seiza* on the wooden floorboards of a breezeway, polished so brilliantly they could reflect a face. Her reddish-brown hair was pulled tightly back with a standard rubber band, and she was wearing a baggy, ill-fitting white shirt and black pants. Her face was plastered with so much gauze and medical tape it covered everything but her eyes, nose, and mouth, making her look like a botched mummy. It was less an attempt at first aid and more a crude measure simply to hide her injuries.

While she had borrowed clothes from one of the white-shirted men—a "live-in youngster"—and changed, she was forced to listen to a one-sided explanation from Yanagi. This was the estate of Uchiki Genzo, chairman of the Uchiki-kai, a direct affiliate of the Okitsu-gumi, the largest yakuza syndicate in the Kanto region. Yanagi served as the Uchiki-kai's assistant underboss. Everything in the household, from cleaning to cooking, was handled by young men handpicked from subordinate organizations, but there was a specific job that absolutely required a woman's touch, which was why they had "scouted" her.





She had been told to sit and wait to hear what the job was, and over thirty minutes had passed. It was dark, with not a single figure in sight. The nearest stone wall was only twenty meters away. If she put her mind to it, she could flee immediately. But Shindo couldn't bring herself to move.

The faint, whimpering cry of that Doberman—sounding so utterly hopeless and resigned—still lingered in her ears.

Dogs were innocent. No matter what kind of monstrous human kept them, dogs were innocent. Those eyes... Even in the depths of despair, they didn't offer the slightest resistance to the one holding their life in his hands. That pitiful groan. All dogs were like that. For whatever reason, they pledged their loyalty to completely hopeless human beings.

She let out a sigh, and her ribs ached a little. It didn't feel like they were broken, but there might be a hairline fracture. She looked back over what had happened today.





For one of her two part-time jobs, doing personal food deliveries, she had biked to Shinjuku and worked until evening. She had briefly returned to her apartment in Shin-Okubo to drop off her bike, wash off the sweat, change clothes, and then head back out to Shinjuku on foot. If it was just to eat, she could have done that in her neighborhood, but she figured she might as well catch a movie while she was at it. However, right after entering Kabukicho, a clearly drunk thug cackled and slapped her ass as he passed by. She instantly spun on her heel, grabbed him by the back of the collar, swept his legs, and slammed him face-first into the asphalt.

It was funny that his buddy didn't notice and kept walking for a few meters, but he soon rushed back in a panic and threw a wide punch. She could have dodged it if she'd wanted to, but since an audience was gathering, she figured it would be less of a hassle if she took a hit, so she intentionally let it connect with her face. Receiving a punch that barely scratched an itch—whether due to his drunkenness or his inherent lack of skill—she grabbed his wrist from underneath and twisted it forcefully inward. A rather unpleasant crunching sensation traveled through her hand, and the wrist joint dislocated perfectly on the first try. A scream.

Panicking, the man started blindly whaling on her stomach with his good hand, so she let go, kicked him away, and put some distance between them. The crowd of onlookers had multiplied in the blink of an eye. The first man she'd dropped onto the asphalt was sluggishly rolling around and groaning, bleeding profusely from his nose. She still wanted to hit them a few more times, but they offered no resistance, and it would be a hassle if the police were called, so she decided to run.

But several clearly rough-looking men emerged from the crowd and blocked her path. Just as she thought, *There's more of them,* a kick came flying at her. It was a high kick, significantly better than what the previous thugs had thrown. She dodged as he kicked a second and third time, shouting things like *You damn bitch* and *You pig*, and caught his leg on the fourth attempt. Her opponent was short. She stepped on his pivot leg to immobilize him, hoisted his caught leg onto her shoulder, and dropped forcefully into a squat, forcing his hip joint into a 180-degree split. A scream like a newborn baby's rang out.

When she stood up, she felt an impact on her back. She'd probably taken a flying kick. Turning around, she saw the man who had kicked her stumbling the moment he landed, so she immediately drove a high kick into his wide-open neck. It made a crisp, satisfying smack. Next, her side flared with pain; looking over, she saw she'd been struck by a sweeping blow with an empty wine bottle. This was probably the injury that was aching now. Being attacked with weapons in such a confined space put her at a disadvantage. Plus, spectators might get hurt. She looked for an escape route, but the bottle-wielding man was winding up to deliver another blow, so she drove her fist straight into his face.

Through the gaps in the surrounding crowd of onlookers, multiple bursts of bloodlust drew closer. More of them were coming. More of them were coming to get beaten up by me. Shindo’s face naturally broke into a smile. *If this were a wider place, where no one would get in the way, I could take on every single one of you.* She cursed the claustrophobia and the sheer number of people in the city. Right then, she took a hard, sharp impact to the back of her head. If Yanagi's words earlier were to be believed, she had been hit with a beer bottle. She took another blow right after, and then, a blackout.





A night wind, unusually chilly for May, blew past. Sitting perfectly still on the cold, hard wooden floorboards, she took another deep breath and gently rubbed her throbbing ribs and the back of her head.
