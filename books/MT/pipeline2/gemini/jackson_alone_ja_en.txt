Skin the color of mixed cocoa, eyes so wide they looked almost demonic, limbs like a black panther’s—he was crucified on the bed. As soon as he saw the video, Jackson realized it was him. He had no memory of what had happened then, and he figured there were probably plenty of guys in the world who looked like him. But this was Japan, and Jackson was the only guy with these looks who would be treated this way.

That morning, the temperature had suddenly taken on an autumnal feel, and Jackson had pulled on a long-sleeve tee before heading to work. Unlike the Athletius track jacket he usually wore, it was from some unfamiliar brand. According to the company dress code, anything was fine as long as it wasn't a rival sports brand.

Next to the massive office building that towered over the sprawling grounds on reclaimed land stood a tiny gym, like a child beside it, and this was Jackson's workplace. At this small, staff-only fitness center attached to the Athletius Japan headquarters, Jackson spent his days giving massages to the employees.

The corporate basketball team, which had been in its off-season until the end of August, had returned, and his schedule had been booked solid since morning. He split the muscle fibers of a forward named Zen with his fingers. Zen’s muscles possessed an astonishing capacity for recovery. Contracted and tight as if rusted shut, the muscles rapidly absorbed blood after just two or three passes of his fingers, swelling with every heartbeat.

"What was your dream when you were in your teens, Jackson?"

"Living a life of leisure."

"Did you ever want to be an athlete?"

"Never."

"Why not?"

"Because I learned how to have fun."

"What a waste. You definitely would've been perfect for it."

*You're the one who's far more suited for it.* Pressing in with his fingers, Jackson didn't voice this. He just stayed silent and continued to split the muscle. 

The conversation had stalled again, Zen thought. Whenever he talked to Jackson, it always ended on Jackson's turn. This elusive vibe—for Zen, that was his overall impression of Jackson as a person. 

What Zen knew about Jackson: He was apparently half-Japanese and half from somewhere in Africa. He apparently used to run track. Apparently he used to model, too. He might be gay. He hadn't heard any of this from Jackson himself; it was all just rumors going around the team.

Seeing as he probably could have been an athlete, or a model, or worked in a more gay-friendly environment, Zen wondered why he was here now, giving massages all day. Strictly speaking, the gym staff didn't work directly for Athletius, and athletes like Zen only used the place for a fraction of the year, so Jackson mostly dealt with regular office staff. They'd complain of ailments indistinguishable from normal aging and then head back to the office. It was probably just a constant loop of that.

Some guys rumored that he only did massage work because he was gay and wanted to touch men, but Zen could clearly tell that wasn't true. He could feel the restraint in Jackson's hands.

Zen occasionally slept with men too, but ultimately he planned to marry a girl and raise kids someday. To Zen, having sex with a man was just another porn genre, in the same vein as BDSM or threesomes. He was never short of partners, and he had no intention of choosing a special someone.

Even without baring their souls to each other, just being fellow non-"pure Japanese" gave them a decent amount to talk about. After the sixty-minute session, the two exchanged LINE contacts; Jackson spent the rest of the morning giving massages and Zen had a team meeting, before meeting up again at the food court at noon.

The food court was almost packed. Jackson ordered tacos and soup and hurried toward the window. All the staff wore Athletius gear, making everyone look like an athlete of some sort. But the real athletes were easy to spot by their sheer swagger. They had commandeered the center tables, cluttering up the area so much that it looked as if they'd been squatting there for hours. Jackson spotted Zen among them. Their timing was off; what Zen saw was Jackson looking the other way. *Still impossible to read.* His impression of Jackson was reinforced once again.

At some point, the sky had cleared, and the weather had returned to the midsummer heat of the days before. Jackson took off his long-sleeve tee, going down to his staff shirt, and crunched his way through his tacos. The chicken breast and grains in the tacos, packed with twenty-five grams of protein, dried out his mouth. Washing each bite down with soup, he finished eating in under ten minutes, then changed the song playing on his AirPods. Suddenly, he remembered the feel of Zen’s back. Even hours later, the sensation came rushing back to him vividly.

When Jackson got up from his seat, Zen was distracted by chatting with his teammates. A woman who, on the night of their first date, threw up the expensive risotto they'd eaten together—completely undigested—all over the bed a few hours later. Nudes sent by a former classmate's mother over a dating app. The whole team was having a blast trading stories guaranteed to gross each other out.

The conversation shifted to Zen’s dating app profile picture. The photo he'd set was taken on a mountain without much of a view.

“Do you even know what a profile pic is for?” the captain asked, leaning in gleefully and tapping Zen’s phone with his finger.

“I mean, this photo is supposed to help people judge if your interests match, right? Is there any girl out there who’d want to be taken to a place like this?”

The way he explained a basic premise everyone already knew, putting on the airs of a talk show host. His hangers-on laughed, making Zen feel embarrassed at what an incredibly lame group they must look like to outsiders, but he just laughed and played along like the rest of them.

The other team members eagerly chimed in. *So true, it’s lame. Like, your eyes are half-closed. Why the thumbs-up? This is definitely an issue. Let's take a new one for you right now. According to some American university study, holding a drink boosts your match rate. And apparently women feel twenty percent safer if you're pictured with a friend. Fine, I'll be in the shot with you. But you absolutely have to blur out my face.* The captain readied his camera. *Okay, look away.*

Only the long-sleeve tee was draped over the back of an empty chair. From within its random black-and-white pattern, a faint square seemed to emerge on the screen. Blink, and it melted right back into the pattern. *What is this? It's reacting to the print.* To the naked eye, it just looked like a streetwear design. *Who was sitting there again? Jackson. Huh. Wonder what it is. Kinda stylish. You think? Depends on where the link goes, doesn't it? Probably just the brand’s Insta or something. Just tap it and see.*

Zen held his pose, coffee in one hand, waiting for their exchange to end. The captain just stared at his screen, saying nothing. The teammates on either side of him leaned their heads in.

*A brand promo? A little much for that. Yeah, a little. Kinda gay.*

Laughing it off, they waited for the video to cut to the next scene, but the shot of the man crucified to the bed dragged on endlessly. *Like, who is this over-the-top gay branding even for? If this actually drove sales, our company would’ve jumped on it ages ago. It’s just pure self-indulgence. Wait a second... is this guy Jackson?*

Zen instinctively half-rose from his seat and tried to sound as casual as possible as he said:

"I heard he was a model before coming here, so maybe it's some brand he worked with back then?"

The other members stared at Zen as if an undiscovered species had just materialized in front of them.

"This?"

The captain set his phone down in the middle of the table, and the members began their conference.

*Wait, by 'model,' you mean THAT kind of model? That's not modeling, that's a totally different profession. Or maybe a hobby. Less a hobby, more a kink. Wearing it to the office? The surrounding staff had all grown completely numb to their racket. A lone temp worker focused on her lunch, suppressing the goosebumps raised by the distinctive *squeak-squeak* of the players' sneakers. A pair of retail staff kept surfing the web. A sales chief with his subordinates raised his voice so he wouldn’t be drowned out. It sounded like a man screaming from deep within TV static, or rather, as if the static itself were made of a man’s screams. When the noise stopped, the food court fell quieter than before, and Jackson raised his eyebrows. In the blink of an eye, he reached the table by the window and picked up his tray, wrappers, and long-sleeve tee.

*Thanks as always.* The one who threw those words at him, dripping with malice, was the captain of Zen’s team. *Your massages are always so thorough, the whole team really appreciates it. Your treatments are really thorough—actually, everyone’s been saying they’re almost too thorough. We can really tell you just absolutely love doing it.*

In apparel, there is always someone who loves playing fashion police with the staff's clothes. At first, he assumed that was why he'd been singled out.

*That.* The captain repeated the word, then made a hand gesture as if reeling something in. Jackson brazenly tossed the long-sleeve tee over to him like a pass. In midair, the name tag pinned to its right breast caught the sun.

Drawing it out theatrically like a magic trick, he repeatedly stole glances at Jackson, who was stepping closer with a look of concern. He couldn't wait to see the moment Jackson's mask slipped.

And then Jackson himself saw the video. His own physique from the past, his choice of clothing this morning, the captain’s malice, and the scheme of whoever was trying to frame him. It was terrifying how flawlessly all these elements seemed to lock together.
