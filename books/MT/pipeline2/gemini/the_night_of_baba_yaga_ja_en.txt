I

Speeding along the Koshu Kaido as dusk began to fall, the white sedan was thick with the smell of cigarettes and blood.

Slumped in the backseat between a man in a loud tie and another in a patterned shirt sat a long-haired woman, her head hanging limply. She was dressed in torn jeans and just a cheap T-shirt, her arms dangling loosely, her hands stained with grime. When the car hit a bump, a thick drop of sticky blood fell heavily onto the belly of her yellow T-shirt. The men kept stealing uncomfortable glances at it. The young man at the wheel, too, shot restless looks at the woman through the rearview mirror every time they stopped at a light.

The sedan was following close behind a Ford. The polished black body of the lead car gleamed slickly under the glow of streetlamps and neon signs just coming on. The sunset that evening was fiercely intense. The glass windows of the buildings throughout Shinjuku were dyed deep crimson, as though the buildings themselves were bleeding from every pore.

Before long, the two cars entered a quiet residential neighborhood in Setagaya. A tidy, tranquil landscape unfolded, so different from the chaotic sprawl of Shinjuku that it was hard to believe they were in the same city. After they took a few turns, however, a high stone wall abruptly appeared, looming menacingly over the street. The tightly stacked Shirakawa stones stood well above head height, crowned with a coil of barbed wire. The cars pulled to a halt before the sprawling perimeter, which seemed designed to conceal whatever lay within. A moment later, a heavy iron gate mounted with formidable security cameras swung open.

Inside the walls lay a massive single-story mansion and a magnificent Japanese garden. There was even a miniature five-story pagoda standing in the corner. The cars crawled along the stone-paved driveway, coming to a stop near the pagoda.

Before anyone noticed, a group of men had emerged from somewhere and gathered around the vehicles. They were all young and grim-faced, dressed in white dress shirts and matching ties.

A man jumped out of the driver's seat of the Ford and opened the rear door. A pair of impeccably polished shoes and the long, slender legs wearing them slid slickly into view. A man stepped out, dressed in a black suit that made him look as if he had just come from a funeral. He was tall, with a cauliflower ear and cheeks so gaunt they looked as though they had been whittled away.

The man jerked his chin, and immediately the doors of the white sedan flew open, and the men inside began to drag the limp woman out. The woman was long-limbed; while not fat, she possessed a thick, solidly built frame, and dragging her out did not look like an easy task even for two men.

The woman was tossed onto the stone paving as carelessly as a piece of luggage, her back giving a sharp twitch. But she stayed face-down, making no move to get up.

"Hey, is she alive?"

When the man in the black suit spoke, the man in the patterned shirt hastily delivered a hard kick to the woman's shin.

A hoarse groan escaped her. Still sprawled on the ground, the woman slowly raised her head like a crocodile. Hair matted with blood clung to her cheeks in a filthy mess; dark red blood oozed from her nostrils, and a gash beside her right eye was swollen a dark, bruised black.

"What an ugly bitch."

The man in the black suit scoffed, and the men around him snickered in eager agreement.

"Get her up."

The man in the patterned shirt nodded, grabbed the woman's left arm, and hauled her up. She offered no resistance, leaning on his grip to stagger unsteadily to her feet. But then—

"*Guh!*"

An instant later, a strangled croak rang out like a toad being crushed underfoot. The man in the patterned shirt was launched into the air, spinning in a full circle before slamming flat on his back against the paving stones.

A murmur of shock rippled through the men. The man in the patterned shirt let out a thin, wheezing breath, his eyes rolling back in his head as he went completely still.

This time, the woman didn't stagger. She stood her ground, planting her feet in an imposing stance. She opened her hands, spread her arms wide, and fixed the men with a heavy, lingering glare. Then she opened her mouth wide, baring a set of blood-slicked teeth.

Realizing that she was mocking them with that smile, one of the men in white shirts contorted his face in rage and lunged straight at her.

"*Tss.*"

A sound like tearing air escaped from between the woman's teeth. She swiftly bent her knees, ducked her head, and charged. Taking the ferocious, boar-like headbutt full in the stomach, the man was sent flying with ease. With no time to break his fall, he crashed shoulder-first into the hard ground. A dull, sickening *pop* echoed as a bone dislocated. He let out a shrill, cracking shriek.

There was a split-second of hesitation, and then the rest of the men swarmed her.

"*Tss.*"

Her fist buried itself into the Adam's apple of a man lunging from the right. Unable to speak or breathe, he collapsed onto his backside, his legs kicking wildly in the dirt. A steel-toed boot drove straight into the kneecap of a man rushing in from the left. The sickening crunch of a snapping joint rang out clearly, even over the roar of shouting men. Another man's fist smashed violently into her cheek. The woman staggered, stumbling backward a few steps, but instantly regained her footing and threw up both arms to guard her head like a boxer.

"*Tss.*"

She blocked her attacker's next swing with her forearm, simultaneously driving her steel-reinforced toe straight up into his groin. Screams. Angry roars. A horde of white-shirted men began swarming the front of the garage.

Flushed red with anger and adrenaline, every one of the men wore a look of dazed disbelief, as if they were trapped in a dream. As if they simply couldn't process or believe the reality of what was happening right before their eyes. Amidst the rage and bewilderment, only the blood-soaked woman was smiling, her teeth fully bared. She just kept smiling, relentlessly punching and kicking without a second's pause.

A dog’s bark rang out, mingling with deep, angry shouts. A black-and-brown blur lunged through a gap in the mob of white shirts. A massive Doberman wearing a thick black leather collar leapt straight at the woman. Taking the forty-kilo charge head-on, she went down. The men instantly swarmed her, moving to pin her to the ground. The snarling of man and beast. The sound of tearing cloth.

"Hey, don't kill her. She's a present for the boss. Handle with care."

The man in the black suit watched with amusement. The sun went down, and night fell.

In the center of an elegant garden spread with fine gravel, the woman was pinned to the earth by four sasumata poles. The men holding them hadn't come out unscathed either; scowling with sheer hatred, they glared down at the woman sprawled on the ground. Her clothes and hair were even more ragged now, but she neither moved nor moaned; she just lay face-down, breathing quietly. Stripped of her shoes, her black underwear exposed beneath her shredded T-shirt, she looked like a wounded tiger. The splash of a leaping koi echoed through the evening quiet.

"Yanagi, what is that? A woman?"

A booze-roughened voice rang out. A man in a striped yukata sat on a leather sofa on the veranda overlooking the garden. He was in his mid-sixties, bald, with a heavy, jutting gut. His short bull neck sank into his sloping shoulders.

"She appears to be a female, at any rate. I brought her in thinking she might be useful for that job."

Yanagi, the man in the black suit, pulled a worn orange leather wallet from his pocket and slipped out a driver's license. 

"Shindo, Yoriko... Twenty-two years old. A Hokkaido native."

"Who is she?"

"There was a racket out in front of the office, so I sent the boys to check it out, and found this thing going wild. She was a rare creature, so I scouted her."

When Yanagi spoke, the man on the veranda let out a grating laugh. The vibrant colors of a traditional Japanese tattoo peeked out from his open collar.

"Scouted her, did you?"

"I had to crack her a few times with a beer bottle before she'd accompany us, mind you. I'll dig into her background properly later, but her brawling skills are the real deal. She took on ten of our pros at once and was still standing like it was nothing. If she wasn't a female, I'd take her on as my underling."

"Are you sure she's really a woman? Plenty of 'em out there these days who look the part but still pack a dick."

"I'll check into that carefully later, too."

A chorus of sleazy laughter rose from the men.

The woman—Yoriko Shindo—listened to this exchange in silence, keeping her gaze locked on the man on the veranda. She wasn't glaring, nor was she pleading; she was simply watching him.

"While you're at it, scrub her inside and out and clean her up. Don't bring her before *him* looking like a filthy mess."

With those words, the man on the veranda heaved himself to his feet and retreated inside. Almost immediately, young men in white shirts materialized like stagehands to clear away the chairs and ashtrays.

"Hey, get a hose out here. Turn on the water."

Grabbing a hose that had been dragged over from somewhere in the garden, Yanagi began dousing Shindo with a gushing stream of cold water.

"Awfully quiet all of a sudden. Finally resigned yourself to it?"

The high-pressure stream blasted against Shindo's bloodied face. Shindo simply opened her mouth and drank the water down in thirsty gulps.

Yanagi laughed. 

"You've got some balls. Or maybe you're just too dense to realize what kind of situation you're in."

The water stopped. Though the cold stream had washed away the blood, Shindo's face was swelling up worse than ever from fresh bruises taken in the brawl, making it hard to imagine her original features.

"If you're an idiot, all the better. Listen up, Yoriko. You're going to be working at this estate from today on. Refuse, and I'll kill you right here, right now. Got it?"

Shindo remained silent, giving a small nod.

"Good. What happened earlier was just us playing around. From this moment on, if you defy me, you die. Understood?"

Yanagi flicked open his suit jacket just enough to reveal a dagger in a plain wooden sheath tucked into his waistband. Shindo nodded again.

"Stand down," Yanagi told his men.

At his signal, the men holding the two-pronged restraining poles pulled back, the metal prongs slowly receding from Shindo's body.

"Get up."

Without so much as a wobble, Shindo stood straight up. The skin showing through her torn clothes was covered in fresh gashes and bruises, yet she showed no sign of fighting through the pain.

"You heard what we said earlier. Strip. Let's see if you're actually female."

Shindo said nothing, simply pulling off the tattered remains of her T-shirt and tossing it to the ground.

"Your underwear, too."

She discarded her black bra without a hint of hesitation. A few of the men surrounding her smirked, but far more imposing than her exposed breasts were the deeply defined abdominal muscles beneath them. Her arms, previously hidden by the T-shirt, bulged with dense, corded muscle. Hers was a physique resembling the fierce Kongorikishi guardian statues at the Great South Gate of Todai-ji Temple—a body that looked as though it had been carved from the trunk of a massive tree.

"Your panties, too. That's the important part."

Without breaking eye contact with Yanagi, Shindo began unbuttoning her jeans. She slipped them down, revealing powerfully taut thighs and a large rear clad in black underwear. The men's gazes locked onto her lower half.

The next instant, Shindo swung the wet jeans overhead and lashed them across Yanagi's face like a whip.

"Shit!"

The stiff, wet fabric slammed into his head. In the split second it took Yanagi to brush it aside, Shindo lunged straight at him. Throwing herself against his chest as if to cling to him, she grabbed his suit lapel with her left hand and snatched the dagger from his waist with her right. But in that same instant, Yanagi reacted. He seized her by the hair, jerked her close, and slammed her to the dirt with a fluid outer leg sweep. He followed up with a vicious kick aimed at her knife hand, but Shindo rolled aside and instantly scrambled back to her feet.

"Get her!"

Someone barked an order. Obeying its master's command, the Doberman from earlier lunged at her half-naked body, its white fangs bared. Shindo immediately snatched up her fallen jeans from the ground, swiftly whipped them around her arm, and thrust it directly in front of the dog.

With a vicious snarl, the dog clamped its jaws around her arm through the wet denim. It bit down with enough force to tear the flesh and snap the bones of an ordinary person, its jaws locked in an ironclad determination never to let go. When she tried to shake it off, the dog only became even more stubborn.

The beast locked eyes with the feral woman.

Shindo bared her teeth and let out a savage roar. It was a display of dominance, proving which of them was the stronger creature. The dog didn't release its grip, but for a fraction of a second, a flicker of fear flashed in its large, wet black eyes. But with forty kilos of dead weight dragging her down, Shindo's movements were sluggish. Sasumata poles darted in from all sides, pinning her torso and forcing her flat against the ground once more. At the command to "Drop it," the dog instantly unhinged its jaws and trotted back to its master.

Yanagi raked his fingers through his disheveled hair to fix it, then spat on the ground. He brought his leather shoe down hard on Shindo’s wrist, pinning the hand that still gripped the dagger.

"Are you out of your mind? Every guy standing here is real-deal yakuza. Aren't you scared of dying?"

A profoundly amused smile played across Yanagi's ruthless features.

"Don't speak to me so familiarly, you piece of trash."

"Oh, so you *can* speak."

Yanagi ground his heel down harder on her wrist. The dagger clattered free from her grasp.

"You... why didn't you stab the dog?"

Shindo's face contorted, and she hacked up a glob of bloody spit.

"Nishi, bring the dog over here."

The white-shirted thug named Nishi nodded and hauled the leashed Doberman over. Yanagi scooped up the fallen dagger.

"I'll tell you one last time. As of today, you work here. Refuse, and I'll kill you right here, right now."

He pointed the tip of the blade at Shindo's face.

"Do your worst. I'd rather die than take orders from you."

"Is that right."

With that, Yanagi seized the collar of the dog—which had been sitting quietly, tail wagging—and pressed the blade against its throat. Shindo's eyes went wide.

"—Stop."

A faint, feeble whimper escaped the dog's throat. This powerful beast—capable of killing a human in a single bite if it so chose—now trembled, completely helpless and unable to resist as its own life hung in the balance.

"I can't hear you."

The Doberman tucked its tail between its legs, its high-pitched whimpers of terror growing louder. 

"Stop it!"

Its large ears flattened back against its head as if crushed, its black eyes wide and round.

"So you don't mind if I kill it, then? It's gonna die because of you. Poor thing."

He pressed the blade deeper. The dog's wet eyes stared fixedly at Shindo.

"I'll do as you say."

"What was that?"

"I'll do as you say!"

Yanagi abruptly let go of the collar. The dog gave a pathetic whine and dropped its belly to the ground, looking up at Yanagi with sad, upturned eyes. There was no trace of anger or resentment in its gaze.

"You can crush a man's balls, but you feel sorry for a little doggy? How sweet of you, Yoriko. Listen to me. If you ever defy me or try to run away, I'll slit this mutt's belly open and skin it alive. Right in front of you."

With that, Yanagi slid the unbloodied dagger back into the sheath at his waist.

Shindo sat alone in *seiza* on a covered wooden corridor polished to such a sheen she could see her reflection. Her reddish-brown hair was pulled tightly back with an ordinary office rubber band, and she wore a baggy white shirt and black trousers that were entirely the wrong size. Her face was plastered with so much gauze and medical tape that everything but her eyes, nose, and mouth was hidden, making her look like a botched mummy. It was less a medical treatment and more just a haphazard way of covering up her injuries. 

While she borrowed the clothes from one of the men in white shirts—one of the "live-in junior members"—and changed, Yanagi had subjected her to a one-sided explanation. This was the estate of Genzo Uchiki, chairman of the Uchiki clan, which was a direct affiliate of the Okitsu syndicate, the largest yakuza organization in the Kanto region. Yanagi served as the Uchiki clan's deputy underboss. Everything in this estate, from the cleaning to the cooking, was handled by junior members handpicked from subordinate gangs. However, there was a certain job that absolutely required a woman's touch, which was why they had "scouted" Shindo. Or so the story went.

"I'll explain the job in a bit, so sit down and wait," Yanagi had told her. That was over thirty minutes ago. The surroundings were dark, with not a soul in sight. The nearest stone wall was only about twenty meters away. If she had a mind to, she could easily make a run for it. Yet, Shindo could not move. 

The Doberman's feeble whimpers of despair and resignation still echoed in her ears. 

The dog was innocent. No matter what kind of monster owned it, the dog was innocent. Those eyes... filled with despair, yet offering not a hint of resistance to the man holding its life in his hands. The pitiful whimpering. All dogs were like that. For some reason, they pledged their loyalty even to the most irredeemable of humans.

She let out a sigh, and a dull ache flared in her ribs. They didn't feel broken, but she might have a hairline fracture. She thought back over everything that had happened today.

For one of her two part-time jobs—doing personal food deliveries—she rode her bicycle to Shinjuku and worked until evening. She headed back to her apartment in Shin-Okubo to drop off her bike, wash off the sweat, and change clothes, before heading out to Shinjuku again, this time on foot. She could have just grabbed dinner in her own neighborhood, but she figured she might as well catch a movie while she was at it. But right after crossing into Kabukicho, a clearly drunk punk slapped Shindo on the ass as he passed by, cackling loudly. She spun on her heel instantly, grabbed the man by the scruff of his neck, swept his legs out from under him, and slammed him face-first into the asphalt. It was almost comical how his buddy hadn't noticed and kept walking for a few meters, but he soon came rushing back in a panic, throwing a wild, swinging punch at her. She could have dodged it if she wanted to, but a crowd was already gathering. Figuring it would be less of a hassle if she let herself take a hit, she deliberately caught the punch with her face. Whether because he was drunk or just naturally inept, the blow was so weak it barely even itched. She grabbed his wrist from underneath and wrenched it sharply inward. She felt a deeply unpleasant *crunch* as his wrist joint popped right out of its socket on the first try. A scream. Panicking, the man started throwing wild punches at her stomach with his good hand, so she let go, kicked him away, and put some distance between them. In the blink of an eye, the crowd of onlookers had swelled. The first man she'd dropped onto the asphalt was sluggishly rolling around and groaning, bleeding spectacularly from his nose. She wanted to land a few more blows, but they were pathetic opponents, and it would be a pain if the cops got called, so she decided to make a break for it. However, several distinctly rough-looking men stepped out from the crowd, blocking her escape route. *Great, more of them,* she thought, and in that exact moment, a kick came flying at her. It was a high kick, significantly better than anything the previous punks had managed. He kicked at her twice, three times, yelling things like "Fucking bitch!" and "You pig!" She dodged them all, and on the fourth strike, she caught his leg. Her opponent was short. Pinning his pivot foot to the ground to keep him from moving, she hoisted his captured leg onto her shoulder and violently dropped into a squat, forcing his hip joint into a brutal 180-degree split. A shriek rang out, like a newborn baby's. As she stood back up, she felt a heavy impact against her back. She'd probably just taken a flying kick. She spun around; the man who had kicked her stumbled the moment he landed, so she instantly planted a high kick right into his wide-open neck. It connected with a satisfying *smack*. Next, a sudden pain flared in her side. Glancing over, she realized she had been struck with a sweeping blow from an empty wine bottle. That injury was probably the source of the pain she felt now. Bringing weapons into such a confined space put her at a disadvantage. Some of the onlookers might end up getting hurt, too. She looked for a way out, but the bottle-wielding man wound up for another swing, so she simply drove her fist straight into his face. Through the gaps in the circle of onlookers, multiple hostile presences were closing in. *More of them. Coming to get beaten by me.* A smile came naturally to Shindo's face. *If only I had more room, and no one getting in my way, I could take on every last one of you.* She cursed the cramped quarters and endless crowds of the city. Just then, a harsh, heavy impact cracked against the back of her head. If Yanagi's words were to be believed, she had been hit with a beer bottle. Another blow followed immediately after, and then—blackout.

A night breeze, chilly for May, swept through. Keeping still on the cold, hard floor of the hallway, she took another deep breath and gently rubbed her throbbing ribs and the back of her head.
