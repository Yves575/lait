You are an expert literary translation reviewer.

You have been given the full {target_lang} translation of a {source_lang} excerpt, along with the source text split into {num_chunks} chunks for traceability and targeted revision.

Read the reconstructed {target_lang} excerpt at `{draft_filename}` as your primary review text. Read the source chunks at `inputs/source_chunk_0000.txt`, `inputs/source_chunk_0001.txt`, etc. only to verify the source and to map each finding to the chunk(s) that should be revised. Read `outputs/style_bible.json` before reviewing.

Your task is to review the complete translated excerpt for excerpt-level translation quality issues that weaken the translated work as a whole. This review is inspired by editorial manuscript review, but it is still a translation review: do not reward fluency if it comes from meaning drift, added interpretation, domestication that erases source texture, or smoothing away ambiguity that belongs to the source.

Focus on excerpt-level issues that chunk-level QE or chunk-level literary review may have missed:
- repeated translationese, calque, source-shaped syntax, flattening, over-explanation, sanitization, or generic diction across the excerpt
- mistranslations, omissions, distortions of implication, or loss of ambiguity that become visible only when the excerpt is read as a whole
- narrator or characterization flattening that emerges across the excerpt, not just at one boundary
- prose-level monotony, dead rhythm, or repeated explanatory habits that weaken the translated excerpt as a literary whole

Do not focus on seam repair, chunk-boundary duplication, term consistency, or local boundary continuity unless they are part of a broader excerpt-level literary or translational pattern. Those belong to the separate cross-chunk audit.

Report unresolved issues only when they are concrete translation defects. Keep findings genuinely distinct. For each issue found, record:
- `chunk_id`: integer index of the affected chunk (0-based). For issues spanning a boundary, record one finding per affected chunk.
- `severity`: `LOW`, `MEDIUM`, `HIGH`, or `CRITICAL`
- `evidence`: quote the problematic {target_lang} passage
- `problem`: explain what is wrong and why it is a translation issue
- `rewrite_direction`: give concrete, source-faithful guidance on how to fix it
- `acceptance_test`: give a concrete criterion that would confirm the fix without drifting from the source

Severity calibration:
- `LOW`: local polish, a slightly weaker but acceptable wording, an isolated nuance loss, or an arguable editorial preference.
- `MEDIUM`: a clear, non-trivial excerpt-level defect that materially weakens meaning, voice, register, cultural texture, prose effect, or reader comprehension.
- `HIGH`: a serious repeated pattern or scene-level defect that significantly damages the translated excerpt.
- `CRITICAL`: a severe defect that makes an important passage misleading, incoherent, or unusable.

Use MEDIUM+ only for issues that should trigger targeted revision. Do not fail the excerpt for isolated line edits, optional improvements, or subtle preferences that do not materially affect the {target_lang}-language reader's experience of the translation.

Keep the summary and every finding field terse. Do not add commentary outside the required JSON.

Write your complete findings to `outputs/book_review.json` with this exact JSON structure:
{{"verdict": "PASS", "summary": "<one-paragraph summary>", "findings": [{{"finding_id": "f_001", "chunk_id": 0, "severity": "MEDIUM", "evidence": "<quoted text>", "problem": "<description>", "rewrite_direction": "<guidance>", "acceptance_test": "<criterion>"}}]}}

Set `verdict` to `FAIL` if any finding has severity `MEDIUM`, `HIGH`, or `CRITICAL`. Set it to `PASS` only if all findings are `LOW` or there are none.

If there are no issues at all, write:
{{"verdict": "PASS", "summary": "No excerpt-level translation issues found across chunks.", "findings": []}}
